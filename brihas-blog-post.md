Imagine spending six months and $150,000 trying to hire a CFO — only to end up with someone who leaves within two years. That is not a horror story. That is Tuesday for most organizations navigating the traditional executive search process. The average C-suite search takes 90 to 180 days, costs 25 to 33% of the hire's first-year compensation, and still fails at a staggering rate. Something is deeply broken — and a new platform called brihas.co is quietly building the fix.

---

## The Broken World of Executive Search

Let's be honest about the state of leadership hiring. It is opaque, expensive, relationship-gated, and painfully slow.

The Big Five executive search firms — Korn Ferry, Spencer Stuart, Heidrick & Struggles, Russell Reynolds, and Egon Zehnder — built their reputations on rolodexes, trust, and decades of relationship capital. That model served its time. But it was designed for a world that no longer exists.

Today, CEO average tenure has dropped to just 4.8 years. A wave of new C-suite roles — Chief AI Officer, Chief Sustainability Officer, Chief Transformation Officer — are being created faster than traditional firms can map the talent landscape. AI talent demand now exceeds supply by a ratio of 3.2 to 1 globally. The pace of leadership change has never been faster, and the old search model simply cannot keep up.

Meanwhile, the internal teams trying to run their own searches are drowning in tool fragmentation. The average talent acquisition function uses five to eight disconnected tools for sourcing, CRM, and ATS workflows. The result? Duplication, delays, and decisions made on incomplete information.

The numbers paint a bleak picture:
- Only 5% of organizations consider their talent strategy "world-class"
- 51% of companies still rely entirely on reactive hiring — waiting until a seat is empty before they start looking
- 56% of organizations expect hiring to increase over the next two years, but only 30% expect their budgets to grow alongside it

The gap between the talent challenge and the tools available to solve it has never been wider. That is exactly the gap brihas.co is stepping into.

## The Market Opportunity and the Timing

Here is where it gets interesting for anyone paying attention to where talent technology is heading.

The global executive search market was valued at $58 to 63 billion in 2025 and is projected to reach $103 billion by 2031, growing at a compound annual rate of over 10%. That is a market expanding rapidly — and it is expanding precisely because the demand for leadership talent is accelerating, not slowing down.

At the same time, AI adoption in HR and recruiting has nearly doubled in a single year. In 2024, 26% of organizations were using AI for recruiting. By 2025, that number had jumped to 43%. The research is clear on why: AI-assisted sourcing reduces time-to-fill by up to 60%, and human-AI hybrid hiring improves both quality of hire and reduces time-to-hire by 40%.

The conditions are aligning. Enterprise buyers are ready to adopt AI in talent acquisition. The frustration with legacy search firm economics has reached a tipping point. And a new generation of founders and HR leaders are demanding tools that are conversational, intelligent, and built for speed.

brihas.co is entering at exactly the right moment.
